Boating

Hogenakkal

  • Boating in Hogenakkal is allowed during the dry-season as the falls are not strong enough to disrupt the passage of the boats. This is the main source of income for boat operators. The coracles are about 2.24 m (7 ft 4 in) in diameter and can carry eight people at a time.
  • Freshly caught fish are sold by the gorge and also various vendors selling water and snacks up and down the gorge rowing their coracles is not uncommon. The fish caught include katla, robu, kendai, keluthi, valai, mirgal, aranjan and jilaby.
  • Place:Dharmapuri


    • Kodaikanal


  • The Princess of Hill Stations, Kodaikanal, is one of the most ethereal hill towns in India. The perfect destination to enjoy an amalgamation of various trees, greenery, hills, cascading waterfalls, virgin lakes and clement weather, Kodaikanal is a destination that you must visit atleast once.
  • The hill station highlights three beautiful lakes that glorify its tourism experience - Kodaikanal Lake, Mannavanur Lake and Berijam Lake. They are distinguished by their charming surroundings and lush greenery. The population of cypress, acacia and eucalyptus beautify the landscape, making for mesmerising panoramas.
  • Place:Kodaikanal

    • Ooty

  • The temperature is anywhere between 10°C to 22°C in between the months of October to June. December and January are cold and chilling. But you can surely engage in outdoor activities in these two months as well. October is the month when boating in Ooty Lake is at its peak.
  • The heavy rains in the months of August and September, leaving behind gushing lakes and lush greenery all around the place. Making it absolutely perfect for the people to enjoy a wonderful experience in the pristine lake.
  • Place:Ooty


    • Pulicat


  • One of the major commercial hubs in South India, Chennai is a great destination to shop for a wide range of goods. Rosewood and sandalwood carvings are other popular exports from Chennai.
  • Great boating opportunities are available in the lake's surrounding villages, where fishermen are always willing to take you out on the water in their traditional boats.
  • Place:Pulicat
